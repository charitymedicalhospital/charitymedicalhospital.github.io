Charity Medical Hospital is a 31-bed critical access hospital nestled in Nyahururu town in Laikipia County.

We are committed to be a leading healthcare provider in East and Central Africa and benchmark against the highest attainable world standards seeking continuous improvement.

Our staff prides itself on the quality and caring patient attention that is world renown. So, come and enjoy the conveniences and care that we provide.

Services

Charity Medical offers most medical services that are available in the market

Some of them include:

Dental Services

The hospital provides general, specialist and emergency dental care.

General dental services include: Check-ups and advice, cleaning, fillings, extractions, endodontics and dentures.

The special dental services provided are: orthodontics, oral and maxillofacial surgery, periodontics, prosthodontics, paediatric dentistry and oral medicine.

Optical Services

The hospital has ophthalmic consultants, supported by a wide range of staff including orthoptists, optometrists, pharmacists and nurse practitioners.

New referrals are predominantly seen in rapid access clinics and general ophthalmic clinics. These are then channelled through to sub-specialist ophthalmic clinics providing state of the art treatment for any condition that can affect the eye.

Imaging Services

The hospital offers the following imaging services:

  • CT Scan: uses X-Rays and a computer to generate detailed images of the inside of the body.
  • Echocardiography: uses standard two-dimensional, three-dimensional, and Doppler ultrasound to create images of the heart.
  • Digital X-Ray: used to view the internal structure of the body.
  • Mammography: used for early detection of breast cancer.
  • Ultrasound

Ambulance Services

Our ambulance is state-of-the-art, fully equipped with all medical necessities and a 24-hour driver and ambulance nurse. The ambulance is utilized by the hospital’s patients and can also be used for rescue missions of other people at a small fee.

General Surgery

General surgery is a surgical specialty that focuses on abdominal contents including esophagus, stomach, small bowel, colon, liver, pancreas, gallbladder and bile ducts, and often the thyroid gland. It also deal with diseases involving the skin, breast, soft tissue, and hernias.

Some of the surgeries conducted include:

  • Trauma surgery is a surgical specialty that treats physical injuries, typically in an emergency setting.
  • Appendectomy is removal of the appendix.
  • Thyroid surgery
  • Shave excision is a simple procedure done to remove growths such as lesions, tumors, and moles on the skin.
  • Vasectomy is a minor operation, during which the tubes that carry sperm from a man’s testicles to the penis are cut, blocked or sealed. It is a form of contraception.
  • Tubal Ligation is a surgical procedure for sterilization in which a woman’s fallopian tubes are clamped and blocked, or severed and sealed, either method of which prevents eggs from reaching the uterus for fertilization.
  • Breast Concerns: breast biopsy, lumpectomy, mastectomy,

Emergency Services

The Emergency department at Charity Medical Hospital is open 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. This department is well equipped to handle any medical emergency. From first aid to fractured bones to heart attacks, our professionals can handle your emergent needs.

Immunization Services

Immunization boosts the body defenses to fight infection. However, to be effective against childhood disease it is best carried out at specified times.

Inpatient Care

At Charity Medical, we strive to make you as comfortable as possible while delivering top notch care. We know there really is no place like home which is why we work very hard to be sure that patients, visitors, and staff are all safe and treated respectfully.

If our resident doctor determines your need for admission, you will be directed to the Admissions Desk where you will complete your admission paperwork.

In case of a referral by your private doctor, please remember to carry the referral letter from your doctor and hand it in during your admission. Our staff will then contact your doctor to inform him/her of your arrival.

Laboratory & Pathology Services

The Charity Medical Hospital Laboratory analyzes patient specimens to aid physicians in the diagnosis and treatment of patients. Laboratory testing is performed on inpatients, outpatients, and specimens collected at physician offices.

We are committed to 24 hours a day, 7 days a week of high quality care in disciplines such as:

  • Chemistry
  • Hematology
  • Immunology and Serology
  • Coagulation
  • Microbiology
  • Blood Banking
  • Special Chemistry and Toxicology

Martenity Services

The maternity services team at Charity Medical Hospital is committed to providing safe, high-quality, accessible and equitable care to women and their families. This commitment is to both high-quality midwife-led services in low-risk cases and excellence in maternal and fetal medicine for women with known risk factors at booking, or those who develop complications in pregnancy.

We encourage women to access maternity services as early as possible in pregnancy, as the evidence suggests that early access to maternity services can improve outcomes for women and their babies.

Pharmacy Services

At Charity Medical Hospital pharmacy, good quality generics are stocked alongside branded drugs.

The pharmacy is open 24 hours every day of the week.

It is open to the public who come with outside prescriptions.

Physical and Occupational Therapy

Back and neck pain, joint replacements, balance problems, shoulder and knee injuries…

These ailments all have one thing in common; a person with any one of these can gain relief and healing through physical therapy. Our Rehab department provides patients with the professional treatment that helps restore normal function, improve mobility, and achieve a greater degree of independence. Our treatments encompass all ages from pediatrics to geriatrics.

The benefits of physical/occupational therapy include:

  • Better balance and coordination
  • Decreased pain
  • Improved ability to perform activities of daily living
  • Improved strength
  • Increased range of motion
  • Restoration of normal function
